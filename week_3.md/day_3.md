## Day 3 - First Training and TensorBoard:
On the third day of my internship, I breathed life into the static architecture I had only structured yesterday. Today, my focus was on actively training my model, measuring its performance, and visualizing machine learning metrics with the same discipline used to monitor a live server.

1. Practical Coding: First Training on MNIST
I began training the Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) architecture I built yesterday on the famous MNIST dataset. During the training, I learned the fundamental concepts of machine learning—epoch, loss, and accuracy—not just in theory, but by actively experiencing them. Printing these updated values at the end of each epoch and watching that lifeless mathematical structure progressively become smarter by observing the data was a truly fascinating experience.

2. Visualization: TensorBoard Integration
Taking the training process a step further, I integrated TensorBoard into my script by adding just about three lines of code using SummaryWriter. Instead of staring at rapidly scrolling numbers in the terminal, watching the real-time evolution of loss and accuracy as smooth, live graphs completely changed my perspective. I genuinely felt the massive perceptual difference between reading raw digits and visually tracking an actual learning curve.

3. The Mindset Bridge: From Kibana to TensorBoard
While watching those live training curves in TensorBoard, a brilliant connection sparked in my mind: Just as we monitor system health and logs via Kibana in daily operational tasks, we monitor a model's health here via TensorBoard. The "monitoring" muscle we use is exactly the same; the only difference is the entity being observed. One tracks the pulse of a server infrastructure, while the other tracks the cognitive progress of an algorithm.

Today, I didn't just train a neural network; I discovered how to seamlessly bridge my operational monitoring skills with the world of artificial intelligence. Watching the model actively learn from data through live graphs was easily one of the most satisfying moments of my internship so far.
