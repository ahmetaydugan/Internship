## Day 4 - CPU vs. GPU, TensorBoard Experiments, and the AlexNet Revolution:
On the fourth day of my third week, I experienced the raw power of hardware acceleration and the impact of architectural changes firsthand. Today was all about conducting practical experiments that perfectly mirrored the technological breakthroughs that triggered the modern deep learning boom.

1. Experiment 1: CPU vs. GPU
I started the day by training the same model first on a standard CPU, and then on Google Colab's free T4 GPU. Comparing the training times was a profound realization for me. The massive difference in speed made me realize that the recent explosion in AI isn't just about clever algorithms; it is heavily dependent on the sheer parallel processing power of hardware. Experiencing this hardware bottleneck and acceleration myself was eye-opening.

2. Experiment 2: Architectural Tuning on TensorBoard
For my second experiment, I altered my model's architecture by adding and removing hidden layers. I ran both versions and compared their loss curves side-by-side on TensorBoard. Visually analyzing how model depth affects learning capacity and defending why one architecture performed better than the other based purely on graphical evidence was a great exercise. It made me feel like a true machine learning engineer making data-driven decisions rather than just writing code.

3. Historical Panorama: From AlexNet to AlphaGo
I connected my practical experiments directly to the 2012 AlexNet breakthrough. I realized that AlexNet succeeded precisely because of the convergence of the exact factors I tested today: GPU acceleration, deep network layers, and massive data (ImageNet). Seeing my daily tasks align perfectly with a historic milestone was incredibly enlightening. To complete the panorama of the decade, I also briefly reviewed the birth of generative models with GANs in 2014 and the triumph of reinforcement learning with AlphaGo in 2016, getting a broad view of how the field exploded.

Today merged practical hardware experiments with deep historical context. I now fully understand that modern AI is not just a mathematical miracle, but the result of perfect timing where advanced algorithms, massive data, and immense computational power finally intersected.
