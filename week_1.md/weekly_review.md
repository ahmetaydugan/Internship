## Week 1 Evaluation : Foundations, Victories, and the AI Winter

I have completed the first week of my internship with an intense rhythm, establishing my technical infrastructure while personally witnessing the historical journey of artificial intelligence through code. This week, I didn't just learn modern tools; I relived the same excitement and frustrations of past visionaries right on my computer screen.

1. Technical Infrastructure and Discipline
I started the week with a clean slate. I built an isolated and secure development environment by setting up Python, VS Code, and virtualenv. While creating my GitHub repository, I adopted the culture of writing "meaningful commit messages" from day one. Looking back and seeing an organized and descriptive code history has been a great source of motivation for me.

2. From Theory to Practice: The First Perceptron Experience
After reading Turing's timeless vision and examining how McCulloch-Pitts translated a biological neuron into mathematics, it was an incredible feeling to turn this theory into code from scratch using NumPy. Writing my own fit and predict methods without relying on any pre-built libraries allowed me to fully grasp the underlying mathematics, especially the power of the dot product.

3. Facing History: The XOR Wall and the AI Winter
The most unforgettable moment of the week was definitely Thursday. After successfully training my model on AND and OR gates, the failure I encountered on the XOR gate initially made me believe I had a coding error. However, discovering that this was the famous "bug" found by Minsky and Papert in 1969 was mind-blowing. I learned about hitting the linearity limit of a single layer—and how it dragged an entire research field into the "First AI Winter"—not just by reading it, but by experiencing it.

4. Conclusion and Presentation
I wrapped up the week with a report and a presentation summarizing this entire process. Answering the question "Why can't the Perceptron learn XOR?" through my own code was highly satisfying. Delivering my Friday presentation on "Why did the first AI winter happen?" and discussing modern parallels helped me view the field not only as an engineer but also as a strategist. I am already incredibly excited for next week's challenges.
